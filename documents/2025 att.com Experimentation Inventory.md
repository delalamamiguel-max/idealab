Test Name: Products cards carousel for Watches and Tablets

Control

What did we test?
During episodic events, performance data shows that customers are interested in device product cards and the PLP destination tiles
for watches and tablets.

What was the hypothesis?
If a product card carousel is added, for watches and tablets, there will be an increase in overall conversion to the page.

What were the results? WIN
For overall ATC, there was no significant difference 1.38% +/-4.59% lift (44.63% confidence level)
For overall conversions, variant had a 31.24% +/-22.31% lift (99.83% confidence level)
For link clicks on /deals/back-to-school/, variant had an 8.98% +/-1.93% lift (99.99% confidence level)

What are the key learnings & opportunities?
The introduction of a product card carousel for watches and tablets resulted in a significant increase in overall conversions and
generated more clicks to the back-to-school gift guide. While the carousel did not noticeably affect the add-to-cart rate, it had a
positive impact on both user engagement and sales conversions. This effect is likely due to higher trade-in pricing for watches and
tablets compared to phones, which encouraged customers who were already considering a phone purchase to complete their
transactions more decisively.
Since there was no negative impact on any key metrics within the gift guide, it is recommended to include product cards for tablets
and watches to further support conversion growth.

Test Name: Mother’s day product cards free phone CTA button

Control

What did we test?

On the gift guide page, the “Shop all phone deals” button under the device product cards generates the most
clicks. We want to test if adding another button linking to the free phones PLP will increase CTR.

What was the hypothesis?

If we create a second button under the device product cards, linking to the free phones PLP, there will be an
increase in CTR.

What were the results? WIN

For CTR on shop all deals or shop all free phones, variant had a 47.17% lift (99.99% confidence level)
For Clicks on Product Panel, variant had a 18.41% lift (99.99% confidence level)
For Clicks on Page level, variant had a 1.28% lift (98.81% confidence level)
For ATC Wireless, variant did not show significant difference -1.46% lift (51.27% confidence level)
For Wireless Thank you, variant did not show significant difference 2.77% lift (33.55% confidence level)

What are the key learnings & opportunities?

The addition of a second CTA promoting free phones to customers proved successful in increasing engagement
rates while maintaining performance levels for the original "Shop All Deals" CTA and individual product cards.
However, the strategy faced a challenge: When reaching the "Free Phones" PLP, many customers did not
proceed further. This drop-off may have been caused by the requirements to qualify for a free phone, which
could have created friction with customer expectations. This insight will be shared with buyflow/PLP teams for
awareness. It is recommended that PLP owners evaluate the "Free Phones" PLP experience, focusing on its flow,
messaging, and overall expected customer journey.
With the test showing positive results, we may explore securing DAC sponsorship to support a component
update that allows for multiple CTAs.
For episodic content, we might consider testing this experience again on a gift guide, but with different
destinations that better align with customer expectations.

Control

What did we test?

Historically, adding the word "deals" to any episodic messaging has increased engagement compared to generic
episodic messaging. On the Deals landing page, including an episodic link presented as "Valentine’s Day deals"

What was the hypothesis?

If we remove the word “deals” from the episodic link, we will see no harm to its performance.

What were the results? LOSS

guides. Since episodic campaigns are limited in time, placement, and scope, we will continue to use deals
